About Pressure Units: Bar and PSI

What is Bar?

Bar is a metric unit of pressure that equals 100,000 pascals (10⁵ Pa). Although not part of the International System of Units (SI), bar is widely used in engineering, meteorology, and various industrial applications. It's particularly common in Europe and provides a convenient scale for measuring atmospheric and industrial pressures.

What is PSI?

PSI (Pounds per Square Inch) is an imperial pressure unit based on the pound-force. One PSI represents the pressure resulting from a force of one pound-force applied to an area of one square inch. PSI is extensively used in the United States for tire pressure, hydraulic systems, and various engineering applications.

Bar to PSI Conversion Formula

The conversion between bar and PSI uses a fixed conversion factor:

Bar to PSI: PSI = Bar × 14.50377
PSI to Bar: Bar = PSI ÷ 14.50377
